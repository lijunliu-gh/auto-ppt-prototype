# Deck Brief Example

Topic: AI agent-ready PowerPoint backend for source-aware deck generation

Goal: Create a product introduction deck that can also work as an early investor conversation aid.

Audience: Investors and technically literate product leaders.

Slide count: 8 slides.

Tone: Professional, concise, product-oriented, and clear about current limitations.

Must include:

1. Problem definition
2. Product positioning
3. End-to-end workflow
4. Python planning layer plus JavaScript renderer architecture
5. Trusted source handling
6. Revision workflow
7. Why this is useful for AI agent systems
8. Current boundaries and next steps

Constraints:

- Do not present this as a finished SaaS product.
- Do not invent traction, customers, or revenue.
- Keep the story accessible to non-engineers.
- Make the distinction between planning and rendering explicit.

Available materials:

- README.md
- PRODUCT.en.md
- USER_GUIDE.en.md
- INTEGRATION_GUIDE.en.md
- sample-source-brief.md

Output preference:

- Preserve sources in presenter notes.
- Use action-oriented slide titles when possible.
